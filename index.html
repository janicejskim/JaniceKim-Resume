I am a third year undergraduate student studying Biology at the University of California, Riverside. Currently, I am working under Dr. Carolyn Rasmussen in the Department of Botany and Plant Sciences. Our lab studies division plane orientations in maize plants. I have been working in the lab since summer of 2018. Here, I have learned various experimental techniques including performing PCRs, plant crossing, conducting DNA extractions, and planting kernels. Aside from this, In regards to data analysis, I have gotten exposure to R Studio and ImageJ.

Since my time in the lab, I have gotten involved in two projects. The first project I got involved in was identifying novel genes from a maize field of induced mutations. My second and most recent project includes observing a gene known as DCD1. My team is currently looking at maize plants that have a mutation in this gene. Afterwards, we will be looking to see if the gene is involved in protein interactions using techniques such as immunoprecipitation and mass spectrometry.

Aside from the work I do in the lab, I am also extremely involved on campus in regards to extracurricular activities. I am in the University Honors program which has allowed me to meet and collaborate with other goal-driven, like-minded individuals. I am also on board for two pre-health/medicine clubs. One is known as the American Medical Student Association which focuses on educating pre-medical students about the medical field. The other club I am a part of is known as Hospice Outreach Program Enrichment. This club aims to provide volunteering opportunities in hospice facilities. Such involvement has led me to meet so many people who carry the same interests as me.
